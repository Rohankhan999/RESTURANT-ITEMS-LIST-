CHICKEN KARAHI

Chicken Karahi is a beloved Pakistani and North Indian dish renowned for its rich flavors and aromatic spices. This traditional delicacy is named after the deep, circular cooking pot called a "karahi" in which it is prepared. The dish features tender chicken pieces cooked in a thick, tomato-based gravy, infused with a medley of spices including cumin, coriander, and garam masala. Fresh ginger, garlic, and green chilies add a tantalizing heat and depth, while a generous sprinkle of fresh cilantro and julienned ginger on top enhances its visual and aromatic appeal. The cooking process begins with heating oil in the karahi and frying the chicken until it is browned and sealed with flavor. Tomatoes are then added, breaking down to form a luscious base for the dish. The spices are incorporated gradually, each layer building on the last to create a complex and satisfying taste. Unlike many curries, Chicken Karahi is known for its relatively dry consistency, allowing the spices to cling to the chicken, making every bite a burst of flavor. This dish is typically enjoyed with naan or roti, making it a comforting and hearty meal. Chicken Karahi's simplicity, yet profound taste, makes it a staple in South Asian cuisine, cherished by families and food enthusiasts alike.
