MUTTON KARAHI

Mutton Karahi is a beloved Pakistani dish known for its rich and aromatic flavors. This traditional curry is made with tender pieces of mutton cooked in a wok, which is referred to as "karahi" in Urdu. The dish begins with the frying of onions, garlic, and ginger in ghee or oil, creating a savory base. Fresh tomatoes are then added, along with a blend of spices including cumin, coriander, and red chili powder. The mutton is simmered in this fragrant mixture until it becomes succulent and imbued with the complex flavors of the spices. Green chilies and fresh coriander are added towards the end to enhance the dish's aroma and give it a final burst of freshness. Mutton Karahi is typically served with naan or steamed rice, allowing the rich, spicy gravy to be soaked up beautifully. This dish is not only a staple in Pakistani households but also a highlight in many South Asian restaurants around the world. Its hearty and robust taste, combined with the tender meat and thick gravy, makes Mutton Karahi a favorite for festive occasions and everyday meals alike, embodying the rich culinary heritage of Pakistan.
